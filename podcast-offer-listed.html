THE PROBLEM

Small-cap companies
get lost in the noise.

Listed small-cap companies produce quarterly reports just like the large caps. But without the communications budget, the IR team, or the media reach — those reports land in silence.

01

No audio or video commentary in connection with reports

Large-cap companies run webcasts, earnings calls, and CEO videos alongside every report release. Small caps publish a PDF and hope someone reads it. Without audio content, there is nothing for investors to engage with — and nothing for platforms to distribute.

02

IR messaging doesn't travel

Without multimedia content tied to reports, the company narrative stays trapped on the IR page. No podcast feed. No LinkedIn audio. No Quartr episode. Retail investors on Avanza and Nordnet never hear the story behind the numbers — and institutional interest follows attention.

03

Invisible to AI — no Q&A material to crawl

When analysts or investors ask ChatGPT or Perplexity about a small-cap company, there is nothing to cite. No transcripts. No structured Q&A. Press releases and annual reports are poorly indexed. The company simply does not appear in AI-assisted research — an increasingly costly form of invisibility.

A quarterly podcast solves all three. One 15-minute session per report creates the audio content, the distribution channel, and the AI-readable transcript — at a cost that fits a small-cap communications budget.
